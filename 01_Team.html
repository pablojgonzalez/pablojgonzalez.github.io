Work with us

The Estación Volcanológica de Canarias is a research group within CSIC, Spain’s largest research-intensive institution. CSIC is not an academic institution and does not award PhD degrees. However, we are able to supervise, e.g.,

  • early career researchers to be enrolled in Earth Sciences PhD programs at Spanish universities,
  • postdoctoral researchers (Juan de la Cierva, Marie Curie, etc), or otherwise
  • self-funded researchers or visitors who want to spend time in Tenerife to carry out a specific project.

If you are interested to collaborate, please contact me and come to research in Tenerife.

Expectations for a PhD/MSc researcher

You will generally be expected to create quality research-backed work based on the topic with define together, mainly based on your interests and my capability to supervise that topic (knowledge and networking options). This means that you will need to read and understand the relevant literature, and you will need to be able to implement the algorithms essential for your research. You will also need to be able to evaluate your work, and to compare it to existing methods. In short, you will need:

  • a good literature review,
  • a well-identified research gap,
  • a decent treatment of the latter, and
  • all of this done in a sound, scientific and reproducible manner.

The expectation is that you will do the bulk of the work over a period of 4 years (6 months for MSc). The first and second year is usual to not publish anything, as you need to become a world-expert in your topic and that necessarily takes time (be patient,… more than you might think). Based on your research, it is usual to communicate results in about three manuscripts (one paper for MSc is an excellent outcome). You will also be expected to present your work at a thesis defence, therefore, presentation skills are essential to graduate with a good PhD/MSc. We will practice them on weekly meetings.
